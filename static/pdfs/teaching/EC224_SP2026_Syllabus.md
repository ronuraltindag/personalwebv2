## Course Description

This is an intermediate microeconomics course that provides an analytical approach to economic interactions between main economic actors (individuals, firms, and governments). The course builds on the concepts you learned in introduction to microeconomics and more formally studies the price mechanism, production, and economic behavior in different market settings and levels of information that actors have access to.

### Knowledge
- Consumer maximization problem using utility functions, optimization of consumption subject to a budget constraint. Consumer responses to changes in market prices.
- Firm profit maximization using isoquant and isocost analysis subject to cost constraints. Firm responses to changes in market prices.
- Industries with different cost structures and returns to scale associated with them.
- Welfare, market failures, negative and positive externalities.
- Different market structures: perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, monopoly.

### Skills
- Advance analytical analysis and graphical reasoning using microeconomic theory.
- Use mathematical tools creatively to analyze economic problems.
- Develop a critical approach to economic theory and problem solving.

### Perspectives
- Have a better understanding of market structure and the interaction of economic agents within the market.
- Have a better understanding of marginal decision-making approach to economic reasoning.
